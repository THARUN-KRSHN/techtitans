Guidelines by Role

Healthcare workers have diverse roles, each with specific guidelines to ensure effective and safe patient care. Doctors and physicians are responsible for diagnosing illnesses, prescribing treatments, and communicating effectively with patients about their conditions and treatment plans. Nurses provide direct patient care, administer medications, monitor patient health, and coordinate with doctors and other healthcare professionals. Allied health professionals, such as therapists and technicians, offer rehabilitative care and perform diagnostic tests. Healthcare support staff assist with clinical and administrative tasks, while social workers advocate for patients' needs and provide emotional support. Home care workers help with daily living activities and health monitoring. Across all roles, the core values of care, compassion, competence, communication, courage, and commitment are essential to delivering high-quality, empathetic, and effective care. These guidelines ensure that healthcare workers provide safe, effective, and compassionate care to all patients.
